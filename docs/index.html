1. My Thought Process & Problem Framing
My approach started with deeply understanding the core problem: inefficiencies in commercial banking's accounts payable workflows. I focused on identifying user pain points and business objectives to define a clear, impactful scope.
  • Problem Identification: How did existing workflows hinder users? (manual data entry, lack of transparency, approval bottlenecks)
  • User-Centric Insights: Key findings from initial research about different role needs and urgency of approvals
  • Strategic Choices: Why I prioritized role-based dashboards and streamlined processes to achieve the documented 50% processing time reduction

2. The Critical Role of the Content System
For a complex commercial banking platform, a well-defined content system isn't just about copywritingâ€”it's about clarity, consistency, and preventing errors. My content strategy ensured information is always relevant, actionable, and easy to understand across diverse user roles.
  • Content Questions as Design Drivers: Used "Primary Content Questions" for each screen (e.g., "What do I need to act on?") to guide UI design
  • Role-Based Content Prioritization: Content tailored to user's role (Initiator, Approver, Auditor) to reduce cognitive load
  • Clear & Actionable Language: Examples like "Your payment is ready to be reviewed" demonstrate direct, unambiguous communication
  • Scalability & Maintainability: Structured approach allows new features to integrate seamlessly without degrading UX

3. Applying a Design System Approach
Building this solution with a design system mindset from the outset was crucial for ensuring consistency, efficiency, and future scalability. It enabled rapid prototyping and lays the groundwork for robust, collaborative development.
  • Consistency & Efficiency: Reusable components (buttons, cards, status indicators) ensure consistent look/feel and accelerate development
  • Scalability: Ability to easily expand the platform with new features while minimizing design debt
  • Bridging Design & Development: Shared language and component library foster better collaboration with engineers
  • Visual Language: Foundational elements (brand identity, typography, spacing) ensure cohesive visual experience
